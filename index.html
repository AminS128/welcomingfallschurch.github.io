About Welcoming America

About Welcoming America

What

Welcoming America is an Atlanta-based non-profit organization that encourages local initiatives to welcome immigrants and refugees to communities around the country. A growing number of communities recognize that being welcoming leads to prosperity for all. Launched in 2009, Welcoming America has spurred a fast-growing movement across the United States, with one in eight Americans living in a welcoming community. A non-partisan organization, Welcoming America is proud to support the many local governments and civic partners who lead efforts to make their communities more vibrant places for all.

Why

Welcoming America values diversity, equality, people, relationships, inclusion, transparency, integrity, and leadership, values that many in Falls Church share. Welcoming Falls Church--a group of citizens and organizations in Falls Church, Virginia--welcomes the Welcoming America movement to our community. This work is always important--now more than ever given the noisy political debates that sometimes distract us from our obligation to our neighbors. Together we can have a profound impact in our community, and make a welcoming spirit real.

Who

Welcoming America's unique, local approach goes beyond a single program or service. Welcoming America encourages organizations across the community to work together, to reduce the barriers that immigrants face to fully engaging in society, and to build bridges between newcomers and long-time residents. Welcoming America’s social change model is unique in its holistic local focus–working with local partners, both public and private--to encourage its residents to engage across race, language, and culture, in a manner that benefits everyone involved.

About Falls Church

About Falls Church

Falls Church, Virginia, is a suburb of Washington, D.C, mostly located "inside the beltway", densly populated but with a small-town feel. The City of Falls Church has a population of just over 14,000, and an additional 80,000 residents of neighboring Fairfax County have a Falls Church mailing address and call Falls Church "home."

Falls Church's history bears testimony to the increasing diversity of the United States. The City of Falls Church was a nearly-all-white farming community until World War Two. The history of Falls Church is intertwined with the struggles of African Americans to attain their rights and freedoms, from the period of Jim Crow through the Civil Rights Movement. But Falls Church--especially Greater Falls Church--is becoming more and more diverse. Just as the United States is in the midst of a historical demographic transition, so too is Falls Church changing--and much more rapidly than many parts of the country.

The public schools of the City of Falls Church play host to families that speak over 25 languages in the home, and the number of national and cultural backgrounds represented in Greater Falls Church is even larger. And the residents of the area and its elected representatives are responding to this challenge: the first sentence of the City of Falls Church's "Falls Church 2040" strategic plan states that "Falls Church in 2040 will be a welcoming and inclusive community." It's up to us--residents of Falls Church--to make sure that vision comes to fruition.

Welcoming Week 2018

Each year in September, Welcoming America communities bring together immigrants, refugees, and native-born residents to raise awareness of the benefits of welcoming everyone. In 2017, Welcoming America's 71 city and county members and 115 non-profit members held more than 700 events around the country.

Welcoming Week 2018 will be held from September 14-23 (more than seven days, to stretch over two weekends--and because there is lots of welcoming to be done!). In Falls Church, the City of Falls Church has declared the week of September 16 to be Welcoming Week--a statement of support for immigrants and refugees in our community.

Welcoming Week will kick off in Falls Church on September 15 at the City of Falls Church's Fall Festival. We invite you to come to the Fall Festival to meet representative of Welcoming Falls Church, learn more about this nationwide movement, and join us and our partners to think about how it can make a difference in our community.


Partners

Partners

Welcoming Falls Church celebrates the efforts of many like-minded people in the community to welcome immigrants and refugees. Local government authorities; community and civic organizations; churches, synagogues, and mosques; and--perhaps most importantly--individuals and families who befriend neighbors, acquaintances, classmates---are engaging international residents of our community and making welcoming work. We are fortunate to live in a community where most residents profess positive sentiments towards immigrants and refugees. The Greater Falls Church area is remarkably diverse, and becoming more so, offering us all everyday opportunities for us to connect with the world, close to home. But we can always do more. International residents of the area feel more uncertainty socially, politically, and economically, than in the past. So we invite you to act, to connect relationally, and to cross cultural boundaries in new ways. Welcoming Falls Church does not seek to supplant the work of other organizations. We want nothing more than to celebrate the work of others, to draw attention and resources to these issues, and to mobilize our community to make welcoming work. We ask organizations committed to the good work of welcoming our immigrant and refugee neighbors to contact us.
