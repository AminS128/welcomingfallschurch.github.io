Welcoming Refugees Initiative

This is a public-private initiative between the City and Welcoming Falls Church, that provides partial payment of rental housing and transitional support, for families settling in the City of Falls Church.

Support includes:

  • Partial rent assistance for up to 18 months
  • Move into a fully-furnished apartment or home, with a fully-stocked pantry
  • A support team to assist you in many areas, including job search support, navigating transportation, ESOL classes for adults and children, tutoring, and helping you and your family meet people and get involved in the community

About Falls Church

The City of Falls Church is a great place to live:

  • We have an incredible public school system, ranked #1 or #2 in the State of Virginia
  • Family-oriented community with small schools
  • Ranked the #2 healthiest community in the country by the U.S. News & World Report
  • Monthly international community potluck meals and events
  • Good public transportation and easily walkable
  • Safe, low crime rate

About the Program

Criteria for application to the program include:

  • School-aged children
  • In the U.S. for less than 24 months, with intention to remain here
  • Currently reside in the Washington, D.C. area
  • At least one family member employed or on the path to full-time employment
  • Ability to commit to at least 12 months rent at $1,500 dollars/month (for a two bedroom apartment, ask us about rates for larger housing)
  • Willingness to participate in a free program with our resettlement agency partner, Catholic Charities-Diocese of Arlington Migration & Refugee Services.
