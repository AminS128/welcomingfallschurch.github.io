National Welcoming Week | September 9 - 18

We look forward to celebrating Welcoming Week 2022, as part of the larger Welcoming America movement. Join us for community-oriented events - serving others, celebrating diversity in our community, and engaging immigrant and refugee families in our midst.

Ice Cream Social | Sunday, Sept 11 from 5-7pm

Join us for our annual Welcoming Week Ice Cream Social at Mr. Brown’s Park, at the 100 Block of West Broad Street, Falls Church. Meet new neighbors in a casual setting over ice cream from Lil’ City Creamery. All are welcome, especially immigrant and refugee families.

Annual Food Drive | September 11 - 16

We are partnering with Columbia Baptist Spend Yourself Food Pantry again to begin collecting for our annual food drive starting on Sunday, Sept 11th.

The most needed list of items are:

  • Canned Fruit (100% Juice)
  • Canned Meat (chicken, tuna and salmon)
  • Dried Beans-1 or 2lb bags
  • Hearty Soup (Progresso, Healthy Choice, Chunky Soup)
  • Macaroni and Cheese
  • No Sodium Added Canned Corn
  • No Sodium Added Canned Tomatoes
  • Non-Instant Oatmeal
  • Non-sweetened Cereal
  • Pasta
  • Pasta Sauce
  • White Rice-1 or 2lb bags
  • Feminine Hygiene Supplies (individually wrapped pads and panty liners)

You can drop off these items at our Ice Cream Social, as well as locations around Falls Church throughout Welcoming Week. Drop off sites include:

  • 204 North Virginia Avenue
  • 605 Fulton Avenue
  • 706 Poplar Drive
  • 215 West Cameron Road
  • 122 West Greenway
  • 116 Rolling Trace

We will stop officially collecting on September 16, but direct donations to the food pantry are always welcome and appreciated.

City of Falls Church City Council Meeting | Monday, Sept 12, 7:30pm

Join us as the City of Falls Church again recognizes National Welcoming Week as Welcoming Week in Falls Church. We appreciate the strong support of and partnership with the City of Falls Church. City Council will recognize us with a proclamation, as we kick off Welcoming Week – see here for the resolution again recognizing Welcoming Week last year in Falls Church, and join us on the 12th to celebrate this again this year.

“Shaping a More Livable World Challenge” Fundraiser | Thursday, Sept 15

Welcoming Falls Church is partnering with DAI Global, an international development consulting firm headquartered in Bethesda, for a fundraiser event as part of Welcoming Week. This year, DAI is prioritizing the support of Afghan refugees in their transition to life in the U.S. as part of its annual fundraising campaign. Falls Church resident Bikram Ghosh - a member of the DAI team - is organizing an event benefiting Welcoming Falls Church as part of this DAI program. The event will feature a guest speaker, former Afghan government official Najibullah Wardak, as well as a cooking class. Watch for more information about how to participate.

Falls Church Fall Festival | Saturday, Sept 17 from 10am-4pm

Join us at 10:00am for the formal launch of our Renting to Refugees Initiative. We are honored that Congressman Don Beyer will join us to celebrate how the community is coming together to Welcome Refugees. And come say “hi” any time between 10am-4pm at our booth at the Fall Festival, on the grounds of the Falls Church Community Center, 223 Little Falls Street, Falls Church.

Sunset Cinema | Friday, Sept 23 at 7:45pm

Join us for a screening of Encanto at Cherry Hill Park, 312 Park Avenue, Falls Church, as part of the City of Falls Church’s Sunset Cinema program. Bring a picnic and invite your friends. The movie will start at sunset.

For more information and to get involved, email us and let us know. We look forward to meeting you!

Past Welcoming Weeks

We have celebrated Welcoming Weeks in Falls Church every September since 2018.

Welcoming Week 2021 was our biggest yet. The highlight was the launch of our Celebrating Community: Our Stories | A Film Series, sponsoring a showing of Raya and the Last Dragon at Falls Church’s Cherry Hill Park, then hosted a webinar - featuring Disney’s producer and chief cultural advisor of Raya and the Last Dragon! - in honor of Falls Church’s Southeast Asian community and in partnership with Eden Center.

We observed Welcoming Week 2020 online because of the pandemic, but our Pick Two (or more) activities offered lots of options to get involved and Be Welcoming.

Welcoming Week 2019 saw us welcome Imbolo Mbue, author of Behold the Dreamers, to Falls Church for a memorable program. Many thanks to FCCPS Superintendent Peter Noonan for making Behold the Dreamers a community book recommendation for Summer 2019 and inspiring so many to read the book - helping us make this event a big success.

Welcoming Week 2018 was our public launch. Thank you to everyone who has gotten involved over these past four years, and we look forward to all that is to come!
